Online Bookings @ Impact Hub, Oakland

oakland.impacthub.net

Meet Impact Hub (IHO)

IHO is a rent-a-coworking space for entrepreneurs. They have monthly plans to share working spaces and they also provide venue rentals for seminars and conferences.

The Challenge

`Reduce calls to customer service with better User Experience`

Booking a venue and signing up, renewing or cancelling memberships results in a lot of phone calls to the IHO customer service. Our challenge was to reduce these phone calls by building a better UX on the IHO CRM portal.


Finding the Why.... User Research

We started with trying to understand Why ? , why is it so hard for IHO users to accomplish tasks on the website. We spoke to several users and went through customer support logs.


Aha Moment & our final solution

Simplicity, Intuitiveness was the key. Our research concluded that excessive noise on individual web pages and too many redirects to complete a single action was the key to confusing most users.Our goal was to make the user’s workflow

  • Clear
  • Simple
  • Efficient

Social Rider Sharing on Hertz

hertz.com

Problem Statement

Hertz is a very popular car rental services. Hertz has been seeing a steady decline in their 1-2 day aka short term rentals due to the emergence of share based care rental services like zipcar etc. Our goal is to build a ride sharing platform on top of the existing Hertz Infrastructure.


Finding what matters

Our research conduct that most of the people were unaware of the locations of the hertz, rather than creating a different app we thought to build a peer to peer carsharing service, that both drivers and riders can trust and rely on. We implemented a mobile app prototype that presents our idea. In our design and prototype implementation we emphasize on establishing trust and safety which is the the most important features that users cared about, based on our user research. We tried to implement mobile application that enables renter to locate and rent from nearby hertz locations, individual can select their ride going to the same location.


Our Goal


Process

For our better knowledge we conducted secondary user research, of share based economy, of the existing competitive car services like Enterprise, Uber, Zipcar, Getaround. By comparing with the peer to peer services such as Airbnb, Hinge, Couchsurfing, we tried to identified the strategies and features from this services and tried to use trust and safety from them into our application.

Taking one step ahead from our comparative/competitive research we built google form for survey in which we get 50 responses, we decided to take it further and also conduct 6 in-person interviews. Involving perception and experience with the share based economy.

Progress Hardware

http://progresshardware.com/

Problem Statement

Progress Hardware, a local San Francisco hardware store also it is a great hardware store that values customer service & great products since 1948, is looking to redesign their website and add an ecommerce component to better serve their customer.


Challenge

To build an ecommerce platform which extends the face to face interaction with customers.


Solution

Keeping all the requirements in mind I designed an optimized landing page, with simple, intuitive and clear product search flow and checkout process for both current and future customers. The goal is to design a website to increase scale, customer satisfaction by providing site-wide search capabilities, featured products picked by locals and promotion of in-person service that progress hardware values.


Process

My process began with a thorough competitive and comparative analysis.

Competitive Analysis

Local stores like Cole hardware, Ace hardware, home depot (a big platform of hardware) provides inspiration for navigation structure and commonly search convention. Local business which is balancing e-commerce and in-store traffic.



Comparative Analysis

I researched websites like amazon, fiverr and apple for product detail page, community engagement, visual design and effective searching of products and check out process.



What Do We Dseign For ? .. (KEY INSIGHTS)

  • Trust - It's important that our first time users trust us.
  • Value - We need to add value for our customers, a DIY page adds immense value for an enthusiast. A DIY portal is where learning & shopping takes place together.
  • Efficiency - Efficiency is important, especially for busy customers
  • Simplicity - Simplicity is crucial, considering a lot of our customers might be first timers, with very little knowledge about hardware tools etc


Understanding Persona’s

Andy, my persona, needs a quick and easy access and checkout process, extensive product detail and the option for customer support, with each access to historical purchases.



User Flow, Card Sorting, Site Map

User flows were developed for each persona to help determine the information architecture needed within the site.

Andy- is a contractor and he spend his day almost at the job site. He is extremely busy and instead of shopping at the store, he’d prefer to order his work supplies and tools online and have them delivered to his job site next day. Using the user flow to map out the steps required for andy to make a purchase online. I realized information architecture will be a critical element in this design.


I took a step forward in exploring how progress hardware’s inventory should be organised. WIth the help of Optimal Workshop card sorting software I conduct card sorting with the 40 participants. I learned how to best group the store inventory and develop main product categories on based of user’s mental model.
